About chromakopia

Chromakopia is the eighth studio album by American rapper Tyler, the Creator. It was released through Columbia Records on October 28, 2024, serving as the follow-up to Call Me If You Get Lost (2021). Chromakopia was written, produced, and arranged by Tyler himself.The album features guest appearances from Daniel Caesar, Doechii, GloRilla, LaToiya Williams, Lil Wayne, Childish Gambino, Solange, Lola Young, Santigold, Schoolboy Q, Sexyy Red, Thundercat, and Teezo Touchdown, with physical releases of the album additionally featuring Playboi Carti. The album blends different genres like hip-hop, jazz, and soul, evocative of Tyler's previous releases Flower Boy (2017) and Igor (2019). Conceptually, Chromakopia is narrated by Tyler's mother, Bonita Smith, as a diary that pieces together Tyler's perspective on past timeline experiences separated by tracks. The album received widespread acclaim from critics, who praised the lyricism, cohesiveness, and production, and even noted its messy and confusing nature as a positive.

    Tracklist

  • Track 1.

    St. Chroma (featuring Daniel Caesar)

    The meaning of “ST. CHROMA” is multi-layered, encompassing themes of personal growth, artistic evolution and unwavering self-belief. The lyrics chronicle Tyler's journey from humble beginnings to his current success, while revealing a vulnerable side that seeks validation and connection.

  • Track 2.

    Rah Tah Tah

    "RAH TAH TAH" by Tyler, the Creator is an energetic and aggressive track that showcases themes of confidence, individuality, and self-expression. The song features bold, often playful lyrics that emphasize embracing one’s unique persona and not conforming to societal expectations. The catchy, repetitive nature of the chorus and the production reflect a sense of rebelliousness and celebration of one’s identity and creativity. Overall, it's an anthem for self-assertion and unapologetic authenticity.

  • Track 3.

    Noid

    "Noid" by Tyler, the Creator explores themes of paranoia, anxiety, and the struggles of mental health. Through vivid, introspective lyrics, Tyler reflects on feelings of isolation and the pressures of fame while showcasing a blend of vulnerability and strength. The song captures a sense of urgency and conflict, highlighting both personal and societal challenges.

  • Track 4.

    Darling, I (featuring Teezo Touchdown)

    "Darling, I" by Tyler, the Creator delves into themes of love, vulnerability, and emotional connection. The song features introspective lyrics that express Ty$'s feelings toward a romantic partner, addressing both the joys and struggles of being in a relationship. Through its heartfelt delivery, Tyler captures the complexity of love and the desire for understanding and closeness.

  • Track 5.

    Hey Jane

    On “Hey Jane” Tyler, the Creator tells the story of an unplanned pregnancy. The song is structured by showing the two different perspectives of Tyler and Jane, the person who is pregnant, as they talk through what to do. Emotions (“still in shock”), the reality of pregnancy (“you gotta deal with all the mental and physical change”), uncertainty (“That is not a good foundation to have kids with / Or maybe it is”) and, ultimately reproductive freedom (“It’s your choice at the end of the day / Just know I’ll support either way, no pressure”) are all brought up.

  • Track 6.

    I Killed You

    In "I Killed You," Tyler lyrically explores the struggle against external forces that oppress Black identity and promote cultural assimilation. He uses hair as a powerful metaphor, symbolizing the pressure to conform to Western beauty standards. The repeated phrase “bitch, I killed you” highlights the act of sacrificing one’s natural appearance for social acceptance, a common experience for many Black individuals. The lyrics reflect how this pressure is often instilled from an early age and passed down through generations, ultimately undermining self-esteem and cultural pride.

    Sticky (featuring GloRilla, Sexyy Red and Lil Wayne)

    Lyrically, Tyler’s verses oscillate between humor, self-confidence, and biting commentary. In one moment, he’s talking about dismissing pronoun politics, and in the next, he’s dropping slick lines about money, power, and fame. The chorus, "It's gettin' sticky, sticky, sticky," is a recurring, hypnotic hook that latches onto the listener, playing into the track's fun and chaotic energy.   • Track 9.

    Take Your Mask Off (featuring Daniel Caesar and LaToiya Williams)

    Daniel Caesar's contribution to the track elevates its emotional weight, particularly during the chorus. His smooth, soulful voice enhances the serious undertone of the message: the importance of shedding societal masks and embracing one's true self. The interplay between Tyler and Caesar serves as a poignant reminder of the struggle many face in reconciling their public personas with their private realities. The repetition of the line “And I hope you take your mask off” resonates deeply, urging listeners to confront their own façades.

  • Track 10.

    Tomorrow

    One of the most striking aspects of "Tomorrow" is the recurring line, "Don't you wait on me, I'll be fine," which feels like a message to a partner or perhaps even his audience. It's a powerful declaration of self-awareness, where Tyler seems to acknowledge that he isn't ready to take on the role of a parent or settle down, despite the pressure that surrounds him. The repetition of this line throughout the song reinforces his desire for independence and his reluctance to conform to expectations. It's a tender moment of vulnerability, as he grapples with the passage of time and what it means for his future.

  • Track 11.

    Thought I Was Dead (featuring Schoolboy Q and Santigold)

    Lyrically, Tyler embraces his provocateur persona, taking aim at critics with his trademark humor and self-awareness. Lines such as "I'm a fake-ass bitch, don't kill me" and "I'm a big troll, haha, I'm silly" highlight his playful yet rebellious approach. The track showcases his ability to mix introspection with bravado, poking fun at his own contradictions while maintaining a defiant attitude. His lyrics address past controversies, critics’ expectations, and his journey as an artist, reinforcing his unshakeable confidence and evolution

  • Track 12.

    Like Him (featuring Lola Young)

    The meaning of "Like Him" by Tyler, The Creator, is centered around the complex emotions tied to the absence of his father and the deep impact it has had on his identity. Through reflective lyrics, Tyler grapples with the idea of resembling a man he barely knows, both physically and emotionally. The song explores themes of longing, self-discovery, and the inherited traits that connect Tyler to his father, despite his absence. As he seeks validation and understanding from his mother, the track serves as a powerful meditation on how family dynamics, or the lack thereof, shape one's sense of self.

  • Track 13.

    Balloon (featuring Doechii)

    Featuring Doechii, the song weaves together elements of confidence, vulnerability, and a celebration of individuality, as both artists articulate their journeys through vivid imagery and clever wordplay. Through its dynamic lyrical interplay, "Balloon" ultimately serves as an anthem of empowerment, encouraging listeners to embrace their true selves without hesitation.

  • Track 14.

    I Hope You Find Your Way Home

    The meaning of "I Hope You Find Your Way Home" by Tyler, The Creator is a poignant exploration of longing, vulnerability, and the journey toward self-discovery and belonging. The song serves as both a reflection on his personal evolution and a heartfelt message to others navigating their paths. With a refrain that repeats the wish for a homecoming, Tyler moves from celebrating a transient lifestyle in his previous work, Call Me If You Get Lost, to expressing a desire for stability and connection. The lyrics encapsulate his struggles with identity, relationships, and the weight of responsibilities, revealing a complex interplay between pride and vulnerability as he seeks to redefine what "home" means in a broader, metaphorical sense

Goblin

Goblin is Tyler, The Creator’s major label debut and second album overall, released on May 10, 2011. The album features the rapper’s most controversial lines that involve homophobia and misogyny, such as in the song “Tron Cat,” in which Tyler raps: Victim, victim, honey, you’re my fifth one Honey on that topping when I stuff you in my system Rape a pregnant bitch and tell my friends I had a threesome As well as the album featuring Tyler’s breakout single, “Yonkers.”

  • Genre: Hardcore Hip Hop, West Coast Hip Hop
  • Release: May 10, 2011

Wolf

Two years after Goblin, the Odd Future ringleader returns with his third studio album Wolf. Heavy on gorgeous instrumentals, Tyler takes a lyrical focus that takes aim at the band’s critics and the trappings of fame. While this album was released last chronologically, there are strong hints that the story laid out in the record takes place before the events in Bastard and Goblin. See this article by Ryan Bassil for the full story.

  • Genreres: Hardcore Hip Hop, Jazz Rap, Neo-Soul, Experimental Hip Hop
  • Release: April 2, 2013

Cherry Bomb

Cherry Bomb, Tyler’s fourth studio album released on April 13, 2015, is Tyler’s personal favorite off of his discography. The entire album was produced by Tyler himself, with some additionational production by Mike Einziger on two tracks. Cherry Bomb is easily Tyler’s happiest and most positive album. Preceded by the dual singles “FUCKING YOUNG” and “DEATHCAMP,” which sound ominous but are actually quite upbeat, the record found favor with critics, but less so with fans. There were still some lyrically dark moments. During “PILOT” Tyler allows his bad guy persona to manifest itself into physical harm, but tracks like “RUN” exemplified his newfound positivity, as he chastises his competition for wasting their young lives with substances and materialism.

  • Genreres: Experimental Hip Hop, Hardcore Hip Hop, Industrial Hip Hop, Jazz Rap, Alternative R&B, Neo-Soul
  • Release: April 13, 2015

Flower Boy

Flower Boy, also known as Scum Fuck Flower Boy, is the fifth studio album by American recording artist Tyler, The Creator. It was officially released on July 21, 2017 by Columbia Records. The album was solely produced by Tyler, but features contributions from artists such as Frank Ocean, Rex Orange County and A$AP Rocky. The album revolves around themes and topics such as depression, loneliness, sexuality, love, friendship, materialism and fame.

  • Genreres: Hip Hop, Neo-Soul, Synth Funk, Alternative R&B, Pop Rap, Neo-Psychedelia
  • Release: July 21, 2017

IGOR

IGOR (pronounced EEE-GORE), released on May 17, 2019, is Tyler, the Creator’s sixth studio album. The announcement of the album came after some speculations the week before, followed by the release of four snippets on Tyler, The Creator’s social media platforms. The album contains features from artists such as Kanye West, Charlie Wilson, CeeLo Green, Solange, Lil Uzi Vert, and Playboi Carti and includes numerous vocal samples from comedian Jerrod Carmichael, although none are credited as features.

  • Genreres: Neo-Soul, Alternative R&B, Synth Funk, Neo-Psychedelia, Experimental Hip Hop, Pop Rap, Progressive Soul, Psychedelic Soul
  • Release: May 17, 2013

Call Me If You Get Lost

CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST: The Estate Sale is the deluxe version of Tyler, The Creator’s seventh studio album, CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST, an album hosted by DJ Drama. The announcement of the album came as a surprise to many when on March 27, 2023, Tyler would take to Twitter to announce the album title, date, and cover. Tyler would later follow the announcement with the surprise release of the single “DOGTOOTH,” along with a portion of its music video.

  • Genreres: Hip Hop, Jazz Rap, Neo-Soul, Hardcore Hip Hop, Synth Funk
  • Release: June 25, 2021
